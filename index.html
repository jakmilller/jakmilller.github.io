About Me.

I am currently an undergraduate mechanical engineering student at Florida State University, specializing in robotics and controls. I have experience working in two different robotics labs, both which share the theme of using autonomous systems to enhance human capabilities.

So far I've been lucky enough to work in places outside my comfort zone, allowing me to learn from incredibly diverse groups of people across the country. I believe that these perspectives have been instrumental in shaping my engineering experience and are something I will seek out throught my career.

As an undergraduate I've taken the opportunity to gain hands-on experience in fields from rehabilitation to high-powered rocketry. More than anything, these experiences have taught me how to learn, allowing me to pick up concepts and make contributions quickly in new work environments. I would love to apply myself in an exciting workplace where I have the opportunity to use these skills in industry.

Bio.

Growing up in Florida, I always loved reading (shoutout the Inheritance Cycle by Christopher Paolini) and played lots of different sports. This continued in high school, where I spent my time playing basketball/baseball, studying, and working as a farmhand. COVID-19 struck my senior year, leading to a pretty anticlimatic ending. In the meantime I worked construction for Florida's first outdoor alpine snow park (??) and applied to colleges, deciding to enroll at Florida State University. FSU has given me the best years of my life and I'm forever grateful for the people I've met during my time here.

One of the most influential periods of my life was my time as a camp counselor at Sanborn Western Camps in Colorado, where I fell in love with everything outdoors. Here I lived in a tent for months, summitted some of the highest peaks in the US, cleaned thousands of dishes, and led kids on multi-day horseback trips into the mountains. The impact these experiences had on my life was indescribable and something I think back on every day.

Since then I've taken every opportunity to travel and try new things, with varying levels of success. Last summer I was able to do research in Reno, NV which allowed me to explore a lot of the gorgeous scenery out west (including hiking Half Dome in Yosemite!). I'm incredibly fortunate to have the experiences I do so far, and excited for whatever life has in store after my undergraduate career.
